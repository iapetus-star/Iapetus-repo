In a crooked tower on the edge of a cliff no one could pronounce, lived a wizard named Thindlebum the Moist. He wasn’t called that because of his magical prowess (which was questionable), but rather because his tower had terrible plumbing. Every time he conjured a cup of tea, a pipe would burst somewhere else.

On the 47th Tuesday of the month, because wizards don’t follow regular calendars, Thindlebum decided it was time to finally fix the one room of his tower he feared most: the bathroom.

“This chamber of horrors has mocked me long enough,” he declared, staff in one hand, a plumber's wrench in the other. “Today, I shall cast the Flush Eternal spell!”

He had invented it himself, using equal parts ancient grimoires, a cursed scroll he found in a cereal box, and the back label of a bottle of enchanted bleach. According to Thindlebum, the Flush Eternal would permanently cleanse the toilet of all foulness, banishing blockage and odor into the Void of Cleanliness.

Unfortunately, he had misread one vital rune, which he thought said “purify,” but actually translated to “summon interdimensional sewage abomination.”

No sooner had he waved his wand and uttered the sacred words- “Sani-tarium Excretus Totalus!”- the porcelain throne began to rumble.

First, a trickle of green slime oozed from the bowl.

Then came the tentacle.

And then, the eyes.

“By the sacred loofah of Lumbarax!” Thindlebum cried, tripping over a stack of enchanted bidet schematics as the toilet erupted into a shrieking, gurgling horror with twelve dripping arms, a gaping vortex for a mouth, and a suspiciously minty smell.

The creature roared in a language that sounded like someone gargling a wet trumpet.

“NOPE,” said Thindlebum, and fled the bathroom, slamming the door which promptly exploded.

The mutant toilet beast, now fully free, slithered out of the tower and down the cliffside, leaving a trail of steaming soap suds and slightly cleaner grass in its wake.

Within the hour, it had reached the local village of Crustlebog.

Within the second hour, it had consumed the mayor, four cows, and the entire plumbing department, absorbing their knowledge and forming a hive-mind of highly intelligent toilet sludge.

By sundown, it had declared itself Supreme Overlord of Sanitation, enforced with an army of reanimated bidets and hostile loofahs.

Thindlebum, watching all this from his broken tower with a bowl of popcorn and a minor concussion, realized he had made a small magical error. “Perhaps a bit too much Void Essence,” he muttered.

Desperate to fix the mess, he consulted the only other wizard stupid enough to help: Doug the Somewhat Adequate, a sorcerer who lived in a giant boot and once accidentally summoned a swarm of talking pineapples.

Together, they hatched a plan.

Using a combination of magical drain cleaner, a kazoo made of moonstone, and a giant plunger forged in the fires of Mount Flushmore, they created the Suction of Doom.

At sunrise, the final battle began.

Villagers watched in awe and mild nausea as Thindlebum and Doug rode into the fray on flying bathtubs, flinging enchanted soap bombs at the tentacled menace.

The Toilet Beast roared, flinging pipes and toilets like missiles. One exploded into confetti for no reason.

“NOW, DOUG!” Thindlebum shouted.

Doug tripped, dropped the kazoo, and accidentally triggered the spell early. The Suction of Doom activated: backwards.

For thirty seconds, all of Crustlebog was pulled into a swirling vortex of magical plumbing chaos. When the dust settled, the monster was gone… but so was every single toilet in a five-mile radius.

The villagers, stunned and slightly constipated, slowly turned to Thindlebum.

“Uh,” he said, “good news! You’re safe. Bad news… you’re going to need very big buckets.”

Epilogue

Thindlebum was banished from Crustlebog. Again. He returned to his tower and built an outhouse. No magic. Just wood, nails, and quiet shame.

The mutant toilet, however… was not destroyed. It now sleeps beneath the earth, dreaming foul dreams of vengeance and bleach.

Beware your next flush.
