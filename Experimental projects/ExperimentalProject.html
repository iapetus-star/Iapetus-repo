Experimental Projects

Welcome to the chaotic kitchen of my curiosity, where half-baked ideas rise, fall, and occasionally catch fire in spectacular fashion. This is the sacred ground where rules go to die, and logic is politely asked to wait outside. Expect strange creatures, questionable code, and the occasional stroke of accidental genius. I can’t promise consistency, coherence, or even safety, but I can promise it’ll be interesting. Proceed with curiosity, caution, and maybe a helmet.

Emoji Story

A wizard’s magical experiment goes horribly wrong when a monster accidentally gets summoned... through the toilet. Told first in chaotic emojis, then unlocked into a full-blown absurd tale of spells, plumbing nightmares, and general mayhem. Brace yourself for the splash zone.

Second Emoji Story

An epic emoji odyssey featuring a woman exploring the vastness of space, meeting aliens, and boldly going where no emoji has gone before. Includes existential space snacks. I spent way too long figuring out if 👽💃 counts as first contact, don’t judge me.

Ball Bounce Game

A tiny physics game where a bouncing ball ricochets around the screen until you click near it and send it flying in a new direction. Surprisingly satisfying for something that's basically just a very energetic circle.

Doodle Lab

A simple drawing app with a blank canvas and one important button: "Clear." Perfect for sketching masterpieces, questionable stick figures, or proving your mouse handwriting is somehow worse than your real handwriting.

Factorial Calculator

A factorial calculator built for my AP Computer Science Principles Create Task that does the math, keeps a history of your calculations, and even shows the multiplication steps when you click a result. Turns out adding a bunch of exclamation points to numbers is perfectly acceptable here.

Animated Ferris Wheel

A CSS-only Ferris wheel that spins endlessly while the cabins stay upright and change colors along the way. It's a tiny amusement park ride with exactly zero waiting in line.

Accessible Mole Mash

A keyboard-powered whack-a-mole game where every mole demands a different letter instead of a frantic mouse click. Built with accessibility in mind, because everyone deserves the chance to panic when the wrong key gets pressed.

CSS Penguin

A penguin built almost entirely with HTML and CSS, complete with a snowy mountain backdrop and a friendly little wave. Proof that enough `
` elements can eventually evolve into wildlife.

Grade Calculator

A grade calculator I built that lets you create weighted categories, add assignments, track your average, and watch a progress bar judge your academic decisions in real time. The login screen exists only because the project requirements said it had to-I don't collect, save, or send any of the information you enter. It works... most of the time. If your grade suddenly looks suspiciously optimistic, double-check the weights and your teacher before celebrating with your parents.

CSS City Skyline

A city skyline created entirely with HTML and CSS using gradients, shapes, and way more rectangles than any skyline probably needs. It even switches to a nighttime scene on smaller screens, because responsive buildings deserve a sleep schedule too. Built for my Web Design certification, but it turned out better than I expected, so it earned a spot in my portfolio.
